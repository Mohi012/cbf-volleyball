Team selection process

As part of registration, you can choose to be a captain. Captains play a major role in selecting the team and maintaining sportsmanship within the team and beyond. Note: It is recommended to volunteer as captain only if you know and are aware of most of the players and their skills.

Once the registration is complete, we will determine the number of teams based on the total number of participants.

A separate WhatsApp group will be created for all participants.

If the number of captain submissions exceeds the number of teams, a poll will be posted in the participants group to finalize the captains.

Between registration close and team selection dates:

We will randomly select five people, who will remain anonymous, and who are familiar with most of the registered participants. These individuals will assign points (100, 75, 50, 25) to all players based on their skill levels, observed during previous tournaments or practice games. Each of the five evaluators will assign skill points separately. We will then average these scores and round them to the closest skill point value for each player and assign them to specific category/roster.

On the day of selection (assume we need 6 teams with finalized 6 captains)

Captains will each receive 400 points. They will then deduct their own assigned points and use the remainder to select the remaining five players for their team. (Ex: if I was assigned at 75 skill point level, as captain I will have 325 pts remaining to spend on 5 players).

Chits numbered 1 to 6 will be presented to the captains. They’ll each pick one and proceed to pick a player from the roster.

Repeat the above step 4 more times until the teams are full with 6 players.

Captains should plan properly to spend 400 points and fill their team.

No changes after the teams are formed.

  1. Team selection by captain are final, no changes
  2. Each captain get 400 minus captain self points. If a captain is assigned with 75 skill points then he gets 325 to choose remaining players
  3. Each team can have 100 (1), 75 (3), 50 (1), 25 (1) player each only


  4. Each team has 6 players in total.
  5. Toss decides the serve or court selection (for leagues). No switching courts until set point/win
  6. For Playoffs and Final we will have toss for set 1 to choose serve/court. Set 2 teams will switch the court and loosers to serve. Set 3 we will have toss again to choose court/serve
  7. Top 4 will go for play offs (1 vs 4 and 2 vs 3)
  8. No game for 3rd spot after playoffs


  9. All league games (10) are for 25 points and 1 set (point on serve break)
  10. Playoffs and Finals are for 21 points and 3 sets (point on serve break)
  11. Rotation and 3 pass is not mandatory and team can play in their respective spots
  12. Same person cannot serve continuously. Minimum of 3 players should serve (A, B, C) No single player can serve consecutively and ⁠If A starts the serve, the next time he can serve is only after B, C finishes (which is like A, B, C, A … and so on)
  13. Three players in front row and three players in back row
  14. You can legally pick the ball from the leg. Or touching any part of the body is legitimate. It is considered as one touch
  15. Team can touch the ball only three times before it goes to other side
  16. Block is not considered as touch
  17. Player touching the net anytime while ball is in air is considered as fault
  18. In a situation where two players from both the teams are jumping simultaneously and the ball gets dropped, close calls regarding whether the ball was smashed while on the net or in the opponent's court should be ignored
  19. If players from both teams touch the net, then it is Re-serve
  20. Main umpire will stand at the center pole Umpire decision is final in all regards (no question asked and he is not entitled to give explanation. If team keeps arguing they will be disqualified period and with 0-7 points)
  21. Umpire decision is final in postponing / continuing a game under the given circumstances. If any team is in disagreement, then the opponent team will get a walkover 7-0.
  22. No substitutes as we picked a team of 6 only on participant demand. so if in case of injury you need to play with 5 players
  23. Each team can request 2 timeouts per game (i.e. they can request halt of play), this can be any time during the game. Each timeout is 2 minutes.
  24. As it is indoor game we don’t need any rotation of courts from start to end of the game (leagues)
  25. When a team is sending the ball to other side of the court it must pass over the net, even I picked outside the court boundary
  26. While a ball carry is not allowed during the course of the game, players are allowed to pick the ball with palms. Umpire decision is final regarding ball carry.
  27. Line umpire decisions are final. No challenges
  28. Leagues , playoffs will be played in 2 courts. Finals will be played only in 1 court
  29. If a dispute or an appeal is made, the game should still continue till the ball is on ground and umpire will take a decision. If you hold the ball in middle of a play , point will automatically go to other team as well as serve


  30. If two teams are tied at same points then we will consider point rate (i.e. if a team wins the game 25- 18, they get 7 points and if they lose 17-25, they get -8 so on average, their point rate is -1)
  31. If teams are still tied at this point rate, then we will consider head on head winner (i.e. whichever team won on its counterpart during the league will be qualified)
  32. All the teams are supposed to stick to timelines in completing the league matches. If for any reason, team is not available to play the match then the opponent will get 5 points for every 10 min delay
